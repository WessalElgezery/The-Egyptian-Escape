  • PYRAMIDS

    Pyramids are ancient structures, mainly built in Egypt as royal tombs. The most famous is the Great Pyramid of Giza, built around 2600 BCE.They symbolized power and the afterlife.

  • Abu Simbel Temple

    Abu Simbel is an ancient Egyptian temple built by Pharaoh Ramses II around 1264 BCE. It is famous for its four massive statues of Ramses II at the entrance and was carved directly into a rock cliff.

  • Karnak Temple

    Karnak Temple is a vast temple complex in Luxor, Egypt, built for the worship of the god Amun. It is known for its large columns and ancient statues.It was expanded by many pharaohs over centuries.

  • The Library of Alexandria

    The Library of Alexandria is a modern library in Alexandria, Egypt, built to revive the spirit of the ancient library. It is a major cultural and scientific center. It houses millions of books.

  • Qaitbay Citadel

    Qaitbay Citadel is a historic fortress located on the Mediterranean coast in Alexandria, Egypt. It was built in the 15th century by Sultan Qaitbay to protect the city from naval attacks.

  • The Roman Theatre

    The Roman Theatre in Alexandria, Egypt, is an ancient amphitheater built during the Roman period. It is known for its well-preserved seating and mosaic floors, used for performances and gatherings.

  • The Grand Egyptian Museum

    The Grand Egyptian Museum is a large modern museum near the Pyramids. It will display the full collection of King Tutankhamun’s treasures. It will be the biggest museum in the world.

  • The Royal Jewelry Museum

    The Royal Jewelry Museum is located in Alexandria. It displays a large collection of beautiful jewelry and treasures that belonged to the royal familyو especially from the Muhammad Ali dynasty.

  • Baron Palace

    Baron Palace is a historic palace in Heliopolis, Cairo, built in the early 1900s by Belgian millionaire Baron Empain. It is famous for its unique Indian-inspired architecture and mysterious stories.

  • Al-Muizz Street

    Al-Muizz Street is a famous historic street in Old Cairo, Egypt It is known for its beautiful Islamic architecture,ancient mosques,and traditional markets,showing the rich history of Cairo.

  • The Mohamed Ali Castle

    also known as the Citadel of Cairo, is a famous historical site in Egypt. It was built by Mohamed Ali Pasha in the 19th century.It is an important symbol of Egyptian history and architecture.

  • Abdeen Palace

    is a historic royal palace in Cairo, Egypt. It was built in the 19th century and was the residence of Egypt’s royal family. Today, it is a museum that shows Egypt’s royal history and beautiful architecture.

  • Saint Catherine's Monastery

    is an ancient Christian monastery located in the Sinai Peninsula, Egypt. It was built in the 6th century and is one of the oldest monasteries in the world, making it such a holy place.

  • Ras Mohammed Protectorate

    is a national park located at the southern tip of the Sinai Peninsula in Egypt.It is known for its stunning coral reefs, diverse marine life, and beautiful beaches,making it a top destination for diving.

  • Umm Kulthum Museum

    is dedicated to the legendary Egyptian singer Umm Kulthum.Located in Cairo, it showcases her personal belongings,recordings,and celebrating her life.

  • Mummification Museum

    located in Luxor,showcases the ancient Egyptian art of mummification. It displays tools,materials,and mummified bodies,offering insight into the religious of preserving the dead.
